# NAMD Visualizations & Physical Interpretation

This directory contains high-resolution (300 DPI) analytical figures generated by the `generate_eng_plots.py` script, illustrating the interfacial quantum dynamics of the Graphene-TiO2 heterostructure.

## Figure Descriptions

### 1. `energy_profiles.png` (Active Space Energy Fluctuations)
* **Description:** Time-dependent evolution of electronic energy levels across the active space (Bands 280 to 350) over 400 fs at 300 K.
* **Physical Insight:** Thermal lattice vibrations (phonons) induce continuous wave-like energy modulations. The close packing of these virtual bands forms a dense quasi-continuum of states without large electronic energy gaps, easily satisfying energy conservation requirements for electronic transitions.

### 2. `nac_heatmap.png` (Inter-State Coupling Heatmap)
* **Description:** Time-averaged Non-Adiabatic Coupling (NAC) matrix across all active space bands.
* **Physical Insight:** Bright hotspots along the diagonal and near-off-diagonal regions indicate exceptionally strong vibronic coupling magnitudes exceeding **15 to 25 meV**. This confirms that the nuclear motion of the TiO2 framework strongly perturbs the interfacial electronic wavefunction, providing a highly favorable thermodynamic channel for charge transfer.

### 3. `state_populations.png` (FSSH Population Dynamics)
* **Description:** Real-time electronic state populations simulated via Tully's Fewest Switches Surface Hopping (FSSH) following photoexcitation at t=0.
* **Physical Insight:** The photoexcited electron in the TiO2 conduction band (Donor State, red line) experiences a rapid sub-picosecond decay, losing ~80% of its population within 200 fs. Concurrently, the graphene pi-system (Acceptor State 1, dark blue line) captures >55% of the electron density. This dynamically proves graphene's role as an atomic-scale "electron sink" that suppresses carrier recombination.
